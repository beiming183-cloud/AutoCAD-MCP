# Industrial Product Design Gates

Use gates as evidence checks, not ceremonial checklists. Record `PASS`, `FAIL`, `BLOCKED`, or `NOT_EVALUATED` plus artifact paths and revision identity.

## Brief And Concept

`BRIEF_GATE` requires named users and scenarios, critical task flow, functions, interfaces, environment, size and cost boundaries, safety/service concerns, appearance intent, deliverables, and classified unknowns.

`CONCEPT_GATE` requires three materially different directions for new or substantially redesigned products. Compare architecture, silhouette, support, interaction, interfaces, cable paths, process strategy, risks, and unknowns at the same scale. A supplied approved concept may bypass comparison only when its authority and revision are recorded.

## Architecture And Skeleton

`ARCHITECTURE_GATE` requires a component tree, functional flows, purchased-part evidence, common datums, interface table, motion graph, assembly/service paths, thermal/routing volumes, support footprint, and hazard keep-outs.

`SKELETON_GATE` requires immediate front, rear, left, right, top, bottom, and three-quarter review from one parameter skeleton. Reject stacked primitives, implausible proportions, inaccessible controls, conflicting interfaces, unsupported mass, blocked service, and uncontrolled motion before detailed CAD.

## Form And Interaction

`FORM_GATE` requires a recorded form grammar and 360-degree coherence. Review silhouette, mass hierarchy, section change, surface transitions, seam logic, opening integration, edge/radius families, visual weight, detail rhythm, interface legibility, grip/reach, and expected user posture.

Do not pass a plate pasted onto a body as an integrated port or control. Model the opening, recess, retention, cable direction, clearance, and user access appropriate to maturity.

## Engineering And Handoff

`VERIFICATION_GATE` separates these dispositions:

- design intent and aesthetic coherence;
- user flow, reach, grip, visibility, and access;
- architecture, interfaces, configuration, and motion;
- B-rep, bounds, wall, draft, surface continuity, interference, and tolerance;
- manufacturing, assembly, service, cost, and supplier risk;
- safety and compliance architecture;
- strength, stiffness, fatigue, thermal, fluid, electrical, or dynamic performance.

`HANDOFF_GATE` requires native source path, document ID, revision, active configuration, units, CAD/kernel/API versions, dependencies, parameter and interface tables, configuration list, exchange re-import results, `TBD` items, `NOT_EVALUATED` checks, waivers, and external approval status.

Automated output remains a candidate for human review unless an authorized process records approval.
